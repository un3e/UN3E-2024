Scope

Extreme environments and emergency cases often pose unique challenges for communication and networking. In these situations, it is essential to have reliable and ubiquitous networking solutions that can support critical operations and save lives. For wireless networks it is even more the case as they can be unreliable due to their inherent lossiness, and sensor devices or mobile agents often face constraints such as limited battery capacity.

The new UN3E workshop will bring the latest advances in research in ubiquitous networking for extreme environments and emergency cases.

    Authors guidelines

    Authors are invited to submit original, unpublished manuscripts demonstrating current research related to the aforementioned topics of interest.
    All submissions should be written in English and have a maximum of six (6) printed pages, including figures and references. Please format your paper according to the guidelines of Procedia Computer Science, MS Word Template, Latex, Template Generic, Elsevier.

    All the accepted papers will be published by Elsevier Science in the open-access Procedia Computer Science series on-line. Procedia Computer Science is hosted by Elsevier on www.Elsevier.com and on Elsevier content platform ScienceDirect (www.sciencedirect.com), and will be freely available worldwide.

    For more information, please contact any of the TPC chairs
